The Foundation & The Scars

Much of the Empire's early history remains shrouded in the fog of forgotten books and private royal diaries. What follows is the official record taught within Padineon: the chronicle of a savior born from trauma.

The Night of Tragedy

The Founder appeared as a wanderer in a small village, a man whose innovations quickly brought prosperity to a settlement that didn't know it needed it. He refused power until the arrival of the brigands. When the village council, blinded by a naive sense of goodwill, released the captured raiders, the consequences were fatal.

"Never again." — The Founder, looking upon the ruins of the city.

That night, fires consumed the village. The Founder hunted the brigands without mercy, declaring them unredeemable. He seized control, executed the conspirators, and transformed a ruin into Padineon. To some, he was the Savior; to others, a Tyrant. He ruled with empty eyes, acting out of a heavy sense of duty rather than spirit.

The Founder's Legacy

After a century, the Founder grew ill. His final act was to pass the crown to his most trusted commander, leaving behind a warning that would define the Empire's soul: "Do not forget the mud and the pain we come from." He vanished, leaving his spiritual legacy to carry his will forward.

The Dark Years: Celid and Aerin

The greatest crisis of the Empire occurred under Emperor Celid. A sensitive and deeply caring Paladin, Celid's spirit was shattered when his wife, Aerin, contracted a brutal plague. Despite every magical and medical effort, she died screaming in his arms.

Something within Celid broke. His sensitivity turned into a terrifying new logic: Mercy through Death. He believed that the infected were being spared from agony by being killed swiftly. This began a crusade of extermination that revealed the first major loophole in the Founder's Will: if an Emperor genuinely believes their actions serve the people's well-being, the Will does not restrain them.

"See if you can do better than I did." — Celid, to the rebellion leader, before his defeat.

The Crusade ended only when a coalition of neighboring kingdoms and internal rebels marched on Padineon. The souls within the sacred sword itself refused to strike the rebels, abandoning the Emperor who had lost his way. The scars of Celid’s reign still haunt the Empire’s diplomatic relations today.
