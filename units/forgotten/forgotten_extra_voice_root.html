Voice of the Root

Tier 5 · Non-Playable · Summoned

Before the First Name

There is a presence in the deepest part of the forest that predates the empire, predates the pact, predates even the oldest name anyone in the Forgotten can remember. The Forgotten call it simply the Voice — because it speaks, and when it speaks, the roots listen before anyone else does.

When Malicott calls it into the world through the Ancient Growth spell, she is not creating something. She is making something visible that was already there. The Voice of the Root does not travel from a distant place. It surfaces, the way memory surfaces: already present, simply unacknowledged until the moment arrives.

It does not fight. It has never needed to. Its presence on the battlefield carries a weight that is difficult to quantify and impossible to ignore — allied units describe feeling a certainty settle over them, a knowledge that whatever happens, the forest will remember who stood here and why. Enemy units describe something different: the uncomfortable sense that the ground beneath them has an opinion about what they are doing, and does not approve.

The Voice is dismissed at the end of the turn. It does not resist or linger. It simply recedes, the way tide recedes — without urgency, without loss, knowing exactly when it will return.

The Root Remembers

Passive
While the Voice of the Root is present, all fallen allied Forgotten units are restored to 1 HP at the start of each turn. The earth does not accept their ending yet.

Ancient Weight

Passive
All enemy units suffer −15% damage while the Voice is present. Something in them hesitates. Even those with no understanding of what they face feel it.

Cannot Be Silenced

Passive
The Voice of the Root is immune to all disabling effects, debuffs, and damage-over-time. It cannot be targeted by skills that would remove it from battle early. It leaves when the turn ends. Not before.
