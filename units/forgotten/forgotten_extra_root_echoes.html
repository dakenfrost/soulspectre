Root Echoes

Tier 1 · Non-Playable · Summoned

The Forest's Smallest Voice

They do not walk. They drift — pulled by warmth, by wounded things, by the residual memory of places where something significant once happened. Root Echoes are what occurs when Malicott reaches into the network and calls for presence without form. They answer because they always do. They do not understand refusal.

Unlike the Will-o'-the-Wisps, who are newly awakened souls still learning what they are, Root Echoes are not individuals. They are extensions. Pieces of the forest that borrow a visible shape for a specific purpose and then dissolve back when the task is complete. They carry no name, no memory, no continuity from one calling to the next. Each time the spell speaks them into being, they are new. Each time they dissolve, nothing is lost — because nothing was accumulated.

Soldiers who have fought beside them report a particular quality to their light: it does not illuminate the battlefield so much as it clarifies it. Standing near a Root Echo, you see your surroundings not as they appear, but as the forest understands them. It is briefly disorienting. Then it is useful.
