Janus

Tier 5 · Campaign Unit

The Reluctant Heir

He was not given a name at birth. Malicott named him later, when it became clear that he would grow into something that looked in two directions at once — toward the empire that had persecuted his people, and toward the forest that had preserved them. Janus, after the old concept of thresholds. He stands in doors more than rooms.

Born to a Forgotten mother who had been through the pact before him, Janus carries the sap in his blood without having chosen it. He never had the conversation that most of the Forgotten describe as the pivotal one — the moment you decide whether you want to continue. He simply woke up already continued, already part of the root network, already expected to care about what Malicott cared about.

He does care. That is not the problem. The problem is that he has also spent years moving through the world outside the forest, under false identities, watching the empire function. He has seen what the Forgotten look like from the other side. Not monstrous. Not an abomination. Just people who chose something the empire does not understand, and are paying the price for that choice indefinitely.

Janus cannot decide if this is a tragedy or simply the world. Malicott tells him the distinction matters less than what he does about it. He suspects she is right. He has not yet decided what to do about it.

Between Worlds

Base Skill
Janus shifts his nature mid-battle. For 2 turns, his attacks deal Pure damage and he counts as neither Forgotten nor mortal for the purpose of targeting skills or resistances.

Root Memory

Base Skill
Channels the root network. Restores 80 HP to all allied Forgotten units. Janus himself heals for half that amount. Cooldown: 4 turns.

Threshold

Passive
When Janus drops below 50% HP, he gains +20% ATK and +15% damage reduction for the rest of the battle. He has stood at the edge before. He knows how to stay there.

Malicott's Trust

Passive
Allied Forgotten units in the same party gain +10% HP at the start of battle. Janus's presence reminds them of what they are fighting to preserve.
