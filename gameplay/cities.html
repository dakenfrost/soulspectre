Cities & Influence

Influence & Conquest

Each City, including the Capital, projects a territorial Zone of Influence. Any resource-producing Point of Interest within this zone that has been cleared of hostile forces will generate continuous income for the city's owner.

To conquer a City, a player must enter it with their active party. If the City is undefended, it is captured automatically until another faction claims it. If an enemy garrison is present, the defending party must be entirely defeated in combat before control changes hands.

Settlement Tiers & Defenses

Cities are categorized into different tiers. Higher-tier settlements increase the maximum size of the defending garrison and provide substantial defensive bonuses to units stationed within:

  • Lv1: Encampment - Garrison Size: 2 | Base Armor Bonus: +5%
  • Lv2: Village - Garrison Size: 3 | Base Armor Bonus: +10%
  • Lv3: Town - Garrison Size: 4 | Base Armor Bonus: +15%
  • Lv4: City - Garrison Size: 5 | Base Armor Bonus: +20%
  • Lv5: Metropolis - Garrison Size: 6 | Base Armor Bonus: +25%
  • Capital - Garrison Size: 6 | Base Armor Bonus: +30%

Provincial Specializations

Beyond their tier, provincial cities possess distinct specializations that grant the occupying faction unique strategic advantages:

  • Trade City: Generates supplemental Gold scaling with the city's level.
  • Arcane City: Generates supplemental Mana scaling with the city's level.
  • Fortress City: Allied parties operating within its Zone of Influence receive an additional base Armor bonus.
  • Barbarian City: Allied parties operating within its Zone of Influence receive an additional base Damage bonus.
  • Librarian City: Allied parties operating within its Zone of Influence gain bonus Experience.
  • Chaos City: Continuously spawns hostile encounters within its territory every X turns.
  • Mercenary City: A rare settlement that permits the recruitment of random cross-faction units in exchange for Gold.
